# Vietnam Cross‑Border E‑Commerce Macro Environment (2025‑2026)

## 1. Market Scale & Growth
- Total e‑commerce retail value reached **US$32 bn in 2024**, with **annual growth 27 %**【1】.  
- Forecast for 2025: **US$26‑28 bn** (Vietnam Briefing) with **industry growth 25.5 %** and **transaction value up to US$28 bn**【2】.  
- GMV of the four major platforms (Shopee, Lazada, Tiki, TikTok Shop) in the first 9 months of 2025: **US$11.62 bn**, **+34.4 % YoY**【2】.  
- Cross‑border import e‑commerce volume: **VND 7.5 trn (≈US$285 m) in H1 2025**, exports **US$3.4 bn**【4】.  
- Ken Research estimates the Vietnam cross‑border import e‑commerce market at **~US$8 bn** (2025‑2030)【3】.

## 2. Consumer Purchasing Power & Digital Adoption
- Average monthly nominal income (first 9 months 2025): **8.3 million VND ≈ US$336**, **+10 % YoY**【5】.  
- Retail sales of goods & consumer‑service revenue projected **> VND 7 quadrillion** (≈US$300 bn) in 2025【5】.  
- **Internet penetration 78.8 %** → **79.8 million users** (2025)【5】.  
- **Mobile connections 127 million** (126 % of population)【5】.  
- **62.8 %** of digital consumers shop online at least once a week; **Gen Z & Millennials** make up **72.5 %** of online shoppers【5】.

| Platform | Market Share (GMV) | YoY Growth | Notable Trend |

## 4. Logistics & Infrastructure
- Logistics contributes **> 4.5 % of GDP** and is expanding rapidly (FDI, smart warehouses)【7】.  
- E‑commerce logistics market valued **> US$25 bn in 2024**, projected **US$40 bn by 2027**【7】.  
- Government‑backed *National Logistics Development Strategy 2030* drives digitalisation, multimodal connectivity, deep‑water ports, and cross‑border e‑commerce zones.

## 5. Regulatory Environment (Effective July 1 2026)
- **E‑Commerce Law 2025** (passed Dec 2025) becomes effective July 2026 – introduces platform classification, seller verification, livestream rules, and **requires foreign cross‑border platforms to establish a local legal entity or appoint a local representative**【6】.  
- **VAT & customs** updates require foreign e‑commerce operators to register for VAT and comply with new customs clearance procedures【6】.  
- Strengthened consumer‑protection provisions (data security, fraud prevention) increase compliance requirements for cross‑border sellers.

## 6. Opportunities & Risks

**Opportunities**  
- Strong growth of cross‑border export e‑commerce (US$3.4 bn 2025) – SMEs leveraging platforms (Amazon, TikTok, Shopee) to reach ASEAN markets.  
- High mobile‑first penetration and social‑commerce adoption (livestream, short‑video) creates new acquisition channels.  
- Logistics upgrades and government incentives lower barriers for foreign fulfilment partners.

**Risks**  
- Intense “money‑burning” competition among platforms compresses margins.  
- Regulatory tightening (E‑Commerce Law, VAT) raises compliance costs for foreign entrants.  
- Talent shortage in data analytics, AI personalization, and supply‑chain management.

## 7. Key Takeaways
1. Vietnam’s e‑commerce market is **maturing rapidly** – $32 bn in 2024, projected **≈$28 bn in 2025** with **>25 % growth**.  
2. **Cross‑border trade** is a cornerstone: **US$8 bn** market size, with exports already at **US$3.4 bn** (2025).  
3. **TikTok Shop** is the fastest‑growing platform, challenging Shopee’s dominance; official “mall” stores punch above their weight.
4. **Digital adoption** is deep: ~80 % internet penetration, >120 % mobile connections, and a young, weekly‑shopping consumer base.
5. **Logistics** is scaling fast and supported by policy, but platform competition and upcoming regulatory changes demand careful compliance and cost management.
