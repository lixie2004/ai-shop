### 3. NPRA (National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency) – Cosmetic & Other Products
- **Scope**: All cosmetics, including sunscreen, lip products, serums, etc. 
- **Process**: Notification via QUEST3+ portal. Must submit:
  - Product safety dossier (Full GMP certificate, Stability report, Ingredient declaration, Lab safety data).
  - Label & packaging “Monospace” for Malay/English.
- **Fee**: RM 200 per product; additional RM 200 for “category waiver” (if you opt for single brand). 
- **Cycle**: 10‑15 days after electronic submission, assuming all docs are complete.  
- **Audit risk**: High, due to strict safety evaluation and mandatory GMP traceability.

### 4. JAKIM Halal Certification
- **Scope**: Any product bearing a halal claim – food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, toys with halal branding. 
- **Fee**: RM 250‑350 × product type (lower for cosmetics, higher for food & pharma). Extra RM 100 if the product contains halal‑approved animal derivatives. 
- **Cycle**: 30‑45 days, with a pre‑inspection if the manufacturer is foreign. 
- **Audit risk**: Medium to High – JAKIM audits supply chain, ingredient sourcing, processing, and labelling.

### 5. Import & Food Safety (DoH / Trade Ministry)
- **Customs duty**: 0–5 % depending on HS code.
- **Food safety (for food & dietary supplements)**: Must register with MyFood (Food Safety Authority) and have HACCP, GMP, and lab‑analysis reports.
- **Trade compliance**: Importers must possess Import Permit & Declaration of Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) certification.

### 6. Other Relevant Standards
- **MSCS (Malaysia Special Contract Standard) for toys** – test for heavy metals, small parts.
- **ASEAN Cosmetic Directive (ACD)** – Harmonization; a Malaysian NPRA registration is also ACD‑compliant.
- **VAT / Sales Tax**: Malaysia applies a 0 % GST (phase‑out) but excise taxes may apply to cosmetics or pharmaceutical items.

## 4. Risk Summary

- **High Risk SKUs**: 1 (sunscreen), 5 (Tongkat Ali), 10 (Kacip Fatimah) – multi‑layer regulatory route (NPRA/THMP & JAKIM).  
- **Medium Risk SKUs**: 2, 4, 7, 9 – food/cosmetic blend; need separate import + cosmetic DOC.  
- **Low Risk SKU**: 3, 6, 8 – product bundles or toys; simpler certification, mostly JAKIM/default import.

## 7. Non‑Compliance Alternative Pathways

1. **Use an Existing Registered Brand** – For cosmetics (e.g., sunscreen), partner with a local manufacturer who already holds NPRA registration and JAKIM halal. License the formula or rebrand.  
2. **Import via “Registered Co‑manufactured”** – For foods, use a local co‑manufacturing partner that holds MyFood and JAKIM certification to avoid the full import route. 
3. **Market as “Non‑Halal” if product does not claim halal** – This removes JAKIM burden but may restrict target demographic; ensure clear labelling to avoid consumer deception. 
4. **Geographically Eligible Markets** – Ship via a neighbouring ASEAN country (Singapore/Thailand) and use the ASEAN Cosmetic Directive for downstream, then apply local registration in Malaysia – cost & time reduce if local partner cooperates. 
5. **Use ISO 22000 / GMP certifications** – Some ASEAN countries accept these certifications to waive some local import testing, but still require labelling and basic safety docs.
