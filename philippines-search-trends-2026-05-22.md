## 3. Intent Classification (Percent of Total Clicks)
| Intent | % of Clicks |
| --- | --- |
| **Transactional** | **42%** |
| **Commercial** | **21%** |
| **Informational** | **21%** |
| **Navigational** | **16%** |

**Interpretation**
- The **transactional** share is highest, driven largely by the blue‑water categories where shoppers are ready to buy.  These are the priority tiers for keyword and ad‑group optimization.
- **Commercial** queries—searching for product comparisons, brand‑less solutions, and price-sensitive shoppers—make up about a fifth and represent strong upsell opportunities.
- **Informational** traffic, while smaller, can be leveraged with “how‑to” content and retargeting.
- **Navigational** queries (16%) mainly target flagship products like flagship phones, best‑selling apparel, and house‑grown brands.

## 4. Key Takeaways & Action Items
1. **Blue‑water categories** (Health & Wellness, Pet Supplies, Mother‑Baby) dominate both crawl volume and conversion potential.  Allocate 50–60 % of the budget to these tiers.
2. Deploy **price‑discount modifiers** as broad‑match modifiers in account‑level negative sets to capture heavy “cheap” traffic that tends to have a lower CPA.
3. Launch **COD‑enabled** ad groups where TEST data shows a 2–3× uplift compared to no‑COD variants—especially for Beauty & Personal Care and Clothing.
4. Introduce **“best price” + “brand‑specific”** ad‑copy for Indonesia‑style trending queries that also appear in the Philippines market.
5. Use **Seasonal calendar** data (e.g., FED 11‑11, 12‑12, Tết‑approach) to pre‑bid aggressively.

---

## 5. Suggested Next Steps
- **Construct structured negative keyword lists** grouped by color (account, campaign, ad‑group).  The blue‑water categories should have a stricter filter.
- **Expand on‑page optimization** for top landing pages in each category, focusing on clear CTA for the “transactional” intent group.
- **Periodic performance review**: re‑run this report monthly, tracking shifts in modifier usage and intent shares.
