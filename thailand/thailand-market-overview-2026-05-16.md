# Thailand Cross‑Border E‑Commerce Market Overview (May 2026)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
- Thailand’s e‑commerce market remains **high‑growth** despite broader economic headwinds, buoyed by mobile‑first shoppers, strong digital‑payment adoption and an expanding live‑commerce ecosystem.
- **GMV** reached **US $38.5 B in 2024** and is forecast to climb to **US $58.5 B by 2027** – a **≈ 15 % CAGR** (2024‑2027)【1】.
- **Cross‑border sales** already account for **≈ 30 %** of total e‑commerce volume, offering a sizable runway for overseas brands【1】.
- Platform landscape is dominated by **Shopee (≈ 49 % share)**, **Lazada (≈ 30 %)** and **TikTok Shop (≈ 21 %)**【2】.

## 3. Consumer Power & Behaviour
- **Population (2024)**: ~71 M; **Internet penetration**: ~78 % (≈ 55 M users)【3】.
- **Online shopper base**: **40.6 M** (≈ 57 % of total pop)【1】.
- **Average annual e‑commerce spend per shopper**: **US $289**【1】.
- **Payment mix** (share of e‑commerce transactions):
  - Bank transfers 40 %
  - Digital wallets 25 %
  - Credit cards 19 %
  - Cash‑on‑delivery 10 %
  - Buy‑Now‑Pay‑Later 2 %
  - Others 4 %【1】.
- **Device split**: **80 %** of e‑commerce sales occur on mobile devices, **20 %** on desktop【1】.
- **Key purchase motivators**: free delivery (58 % of shoppers), price comparison, and product variety.

## 4. Platform Landscape (2024)
| Platform | Approx. GMV Share | Key Strengths |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Shopee** | ~49 % | Aggressive promotions, deep logistic network (Shopee Xpress), strong mobile UI, dominant in flash‑sales & live‑commerce |
| **Lazada** | ~30 % | Backed by Alibaba, robust warehousing, strong in electronics & home appliances |
| **TikTok Shop** | ~21 % | Social‑commerce + short‑form video, high engagement among Gen Z, built‑in live‑shopping tools |
| **Other (Amazon, AliExpress, etc.)** | < 5 % | Niche categories, price‑sensitive imports |

*Source: Momentum Works “E‑commerce in Southeast Asia 2024” (based on a 680 bn baht market estimate)【2】.*
