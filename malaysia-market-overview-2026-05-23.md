## Executive Summary
- Malaysia’s digital economy is projected to reach **US$31 bn GMV in 2024**, up 16 % YoY, with **e‑commerce accounting for US$16 bn (≈RM71 bn) – a 17 % increase**【source:1】.
- Consumer purchasing power is strong: average household income rose to **RM8,479 per month** in 2022【source:2】; total disposable income reached **RM1.76 trn in 2023**【source:3】.
- **Shopee remains dominant** (≈52 % of regional platform GMV) and is the clear market leader in Malaysia; **Lazada holds ~19 %** share, while **TikTok Shop is rapidly gaining ground**【source:4】【source:5】.
- The **2024 Consumer Protection (Electronic Trade Transaction) Regulations** expand the definition of “online marketplace”, require Bahasa Malaysia disclosures and impose penalties up to **RM100 k or 3 yr imprisonment**【source:6】. Import duties vary by product & FTAs; a Low‑Value‑Goods (LVG) tax has been introduced【source:7】.
- Logistics infrastructure is mature and expanding: KLIA, Central Spine Road, Pan‑Borneo Highway, multiple FTZs and a **single‑window customs system** accelerate cross‑border flows. The logistics market is forecast to grow **3.6 % CAGR (2025‑2035)**【source:8】, with FedEx highlighting EV trials and digital tracking adoption【source:9】.
- Payments are highly diversified: **bank transfers 37 %**, **debit/credit cards 28 %**, **digital & mobile wallets 24 %** of transactions【source:10】. The ecosystem is overseen by Bank Negara Malaysia (Payment Services Act 2013) and is moving toward real‑time payments.

## 4. Cross‑Border E‑Commerce Policy Environment
### 4.1 Consumer‑Protection (Electronic Trade Transaction) Regulations 2024
- **Scope broadened** to cover any “electronic trade platform” (websites, apps, social‑media marketplaces)【source:6】.
- **Dual definition:**
  - *Online marketplace operator* – platform owners.
  - *Online marketplace supplier* – third‑party sellers.
- **Key obligations for suppliers:**
  - Full product disclosures (price, taxes, delivery estimate, safety certifications).
  - Use **Bahasa Malaysia** for mandatory disclosures (enforcement paused until 2025)【source:6】.
  - Cover return‑shipping for defective/incorrect goods.
- **Operator duties:** monitor supplier compliance, maintain 3‑year supplier records, provide complaint channels.
- **Penalties:** up to **RM100 k** fine and/or **3 years imprisonment** for individuals; higher for repeat offenses【source:6】.

### 4.2 Import Duties & Taxation
- **Duty rates** depend on product classification and origin; FTAs (e.g., ASEAN‑Australia‑New Zealand FTA) can reduce or eliminate tariffs【source:7】.
- **Certificate of origin** is recommended to claim preferential rates.
- **Low‑Value‑Goods (LVG) tax:** introduced for goods ≤ RM500, aimed at leveling the playing field between online and offline retailers【source:7】.
- **VAT/GST:** Standard **10 %**; reduced **6 %** for services (restaurants, hotels, etc.) and **5 %** for IT & telecom hardware; **0 %** for basic food/agri‑products【source:10】.

### 4.3 Customs & Trade Facilitation
- **Single‑window system** (customs documentation portal) streamlines permits, licences and regulatory approvals, cutting clearance time for cross‑border parcels【source:9】.
- **Digital customs filings** are mandatory for shipments >RM5 000.

|--------|-----------|
| **Air connectivity** | KLIA (world‑class hub) integrates ground‑air networks; cargo capacity expanding with dedicated freight facilities【source:9】 |
| **Road & rail** | Central Spine Road, Pan‑Borneo Highway improve east‑west connectivity; rail freight corridors linking ports to inland manufacturing zones【source:9】 |
| **Ports & FTZs** | Major ports (Port Klang, Penang, Tanjung Pelepas) and multiple Free‑Trade Zones provide duty‑free storage and processing【source:9】 |
| **Logistics market growth** | **3.6 % CAGR (2025‑2035)**, driven by e‑commerce expansion and digitalisation【source:8】 |
| **Technology adoption** | AI‑driven routing, computer‑assisted vehicles, EV delivery pilots (Malaysia‑Singapore trial)【source:9】 |
| **Sustainability** | FedEx targets carbon‑neutral operations by 2040; EV fleet expansion and carbon‑capture research underway【source:9】 |
| **Government initiatives** | *Pelan Strategik Pendigitalan Sektor Awam* (5‑year public‑sector digitalisation plan); *single‑window customs*; incentives for smart‑warehouse development【source:9】 |

## 6. Payment Ecosystem
| Payment type | Share of e‑commerce transactions |
|--------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Bank transfers** | **37 %**【source:10】 |
| **Debit / credit cards** | **28 %**【source:10】 |
| **Digital & mobile wallets** | **24 %** (e.g., Touch ‘n Go, GrabPay, Boost)【source:10】 |
| **Cash on delivery** | Remaining share (≈11 %) |

- **Regulation:** Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) governs payment service providers under the **Payment Services Act 2013** and the **Financial Services Act 2013**.
- **Real‑time payments (RTP):** Malaysia’s **PayNow** and **e‑Banking Instant Transfer** services enable near‑instant fund settlement, increasingly integrated into checkout flows.
- **Emerging trends:** QR‑code payments, tokenisation for card‑on‑file, and increasing adoption of **Buy‑Now‑Pay‑Later (BNPL)** solutions (e.g., Atome, Hoolah).
